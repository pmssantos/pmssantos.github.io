Experience

Post-Doctoral Researcher in project Generation.Mobi

Work Period: Jan 2017-Jul 2018

Reference/Funding: Portugal 2020 Portuguese funding program, reference: POCI-01-0247-FEDER-017369

Project Description:The goal of the project is to deploy innovative functionalities in electric and non-electric bicycles, such as ad hoc communication and vital sign monitoring. It features as partners the Centro para a Excelência e Inovação na Indústria Automóvel (CEiiA; an interface institute of the Portuguese R&D ecosystem), the University of Porto, and companies Ibérica (bicycles manufacturer) and CardioID (develops vital signs monitoring solutions).

Work Description:I manage and participate in the tasks related to ad hoc communication, overseeing the work of four people. The addressed topics are the experimental evaluation of wireless technologies in the 2.4 GHz ISM band and the identification of the position of the wireless modules in the bicycle (carried out by an B.Sc. and M.Sc. scholarship grantees), and the development of prototype ad hoc applications such as neighbor identification over Bluetooth links and audio broadcast over in a bicycle platoon (carried out by two other B.Sc. and M.Sc. scholarship grantees).


Ph.D. Researcher in project Future Cities

Period: Mai 2014-Mai 2016

Funding/reference: 7th Framework Program (FP7), reference: FP7-REGPOT-2012-2013-1, 316296

Project Description:The project goal was to implement intelligent smart city management systems, and involved the deployment of massive IoT platforms in Porto, Portugal, namely a vehicular network of 600+ on-board units in fleet vehicles (buses and garbage-collection trucks). The main partners were the Municipality of Porto (CMP) and the University of Porto, and spinned off the the start-up VENIAM (that commercializes vehicular networking technology).

Work Description:I participated in the development and operation of a platform for weather and air quality monitoring in Porto (in partnership with the municipality), and in the characterization of WiFi links between road-side wireless sensors and WiFi-enabled vehicular nodes, and evaluation of delay-tolerant strategies for data collection (in partnership with the start-up VENIAM). I also produced a mechanism to estimate the data collection service by vehicular networks to road-side sensors and inform infrastructure planning.


Ph.D. Researcher in project Vital Responder

Period: Out 2009-Jul 2012

Funding/Reference: Carnegie Mellon University and Portugal Partnership Program, reference: CMU-P/CPS/0046/2008.

Project Description:The partners were the University of Porto, the Instituto de Engenharia Electrónica e Telemática (IEETA; a public research institute) of the University of Aveiro, and the company Biodevices (develops vital signs monitoring solutions).

Work Description:I studied and developed ad hoc communication solutions between firefighters in forest scenarios. In detail, I carried out wireless channel modeling from received signal strength measurements in forest settings, developed mobility models of firefighters based on their procedures and testemonies, and designed and developed ad hoc protocols over GPS mobility traces provided by the firefighters. In this process, I worked closely with two firefighter corporations (Bombeiros Voluntários de Amarante and Bombeiros Sapadores de Gaia) to gatherthe relevant data and information.
